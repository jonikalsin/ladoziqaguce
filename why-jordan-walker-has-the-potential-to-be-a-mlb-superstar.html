Why Jordan Walker Has the Potential to be a MLB Superstar

Jordan Walker, a towering 6-foot-5, 240-pound infielder, has been making waves in the baseball world due to his exceptional talent and potential.

As a young prospect in the St. Louis Cardinals organization, Walker's impressive power hitting and overall athleticism have garnered attention, leading many to believe that he has the potential to become an MLB superstar. 

Early Career and Power Potential

In his high school years at Stone Mountain, Georgia, Jordan Walker's towering presence and ability to produce triple-digit speeds off the bat made him one of the highest-ranked prospects in the country.

His power hitting capabilities were particularly noteworthy, with frequent exit velocities over 100 mph and a clear dominance as a corner-infield prospect in the 2020 high school class.

Professional Debut and Performance

After being drafted by the St. Louis Cardinals as the 21st overall pick in 2020, Walker signed a $2.9 million deal to forgo his commitment to Duke University.

His professional career began with Low-A Palm Beach, where he made an immediate impact by hitting a home run on the first pitch he saw in his first professional game.

Throughout his brief professional career, Walker has demonstrated his offensive prowess, showcasing a .319/.391/.516 slash line with nine homers, 23 doubles, and 34 RBIs across two levels.

Also, his ability to make adjustments and handle Minor League pitching has been evident, earning him a promotion to High-A Peoria at the age of 19.

Scouting Report and MLB Pipeline Evaluation

According to MLB Pipeline, Walker has been evaluated with the following scouting grades (20-80 scale):

  • HIT: 45
  • POWER: 60
  • RUN: 45
  • ARM: 55
  • FIELD: 45
  • OVERALL: 55

While questions have been raised about his ability to hit breaking balls and some swing and miss in his game, Walker has shown an improved ability to make contact and a strong work ethic, indicating potential for continued development.

Also, his athleticism for his size has led to optimism for his potential to remain at third base, where he has exclusively played thus far in his professional career.

Dynamic Duo with Masyn Winn

Walker's impact extends beyond his individual performance, as he forms a dynamic duo with fellow Cardinals prospect Masyn Winn.

The close bond between the two young players has been highlighted, with their supportive relationship and mutual encouragement playing a significant role in their development.

Insider Predictions and Future Outlook

Insider predictions, such as those from The Athletic's Jim Bowden, suggest that Walker is poised for a breakout year and has the potential to follow in the footsteps of MLB stars like Aaron Judge and Mike Trout after their initial seasons.

Walker's impressive performance in his rookie season, coupled with the expectation of continued growth in his hitting ability and power potential, positions him as a player to watch in the upcoming MLB season.

 

To sum things up, Jordan Walker's exceptional talent, power potential, and overall athletic ability have laid the foundation for him to become a future MLB superstar.

His early career success, positive evaluations from scouting reports, and support from insiders indicate that he is well-positioned to make a significant impact in the world of professional baseball.

By harnessing his power and continuing to refine his skills, Walker has the opportunity to carve out a successful career and leave a lasting legacy in the MLB.
