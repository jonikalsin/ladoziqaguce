Jake Paul and Tommy Fury have announced that they will be fighting in Saudi Arabia on 26th February, with the American backed by bookies as the odds on favourite for victory.

This is a third time the two internet celebrities have been scheduled to fight each other, though the fight was forced to be called off on both of the previous occasions.

The original fight was meant to take place in December 2021 before Fury was forced to withdraw through injury.

The fight date was revised for August 2022, but again the bout had to be called off as the Brit was denied access to the United States.

Both fighters have unbeaten records going into fight, which has been billed as 'The Truth'.

Paul, nicknamed 'The Problem Child' will take a 6-0 record to Saudi Arabia, four of his wins coming by knockout, with his most recent fight being a unanimous decision victory over former UFC champions Anderson Silva back in October.

Fury is 8-0 following his most recent win against Daniel Bocianski of Poland, which the Brit, nicknamed 'TNT' won on points in April last year on the undercard of Tyson's fight with Dillian Whyte at Wembley Stadium.

The American former YouTuber and social media personality is the early favourite following the fight's official announcement, with Betfair backing him at 8/13.

Fury, the half brother of WBC heavyweight champion Tyson, initially rose to fame after appearing on Love Island in 2019, finishing as runners up along with girlfriend Molly-Mae Hague, and is 7/5 to win in Riyadh next month.
